Description of the Frisco & Plano Branch

AYLUS Frisco & Plano is a non-profit student organization with the sole purpose of promoting young students’ contribution and volunteering to the societies. Our mission is to contribute to society and help others bring out their potential. Here in Frisco and Plano, we aim to better our community by emphasizing on forming a social-friendly environment. We support human development through the promotion of volunteering. As a part of our commitment to both transparency and to creating opportunities for the community, AYLUS Frisco & Plano Branch welcomes volunteers in a variety of roles, including activities that showcase leadership and creativity. This branch is universal, inclusive, and enthusiastically encourages volunteer actions in any way possible. Every member at our organization looks for ways to involve volunteers in his or her work. We aim to involve the community in all aspects of our work and strive to form various projects that will benefit others, as well as create a healthy learning experience. Join our group with full engagement and commitment to help others reach their full potentials and promote a better community!

 

Notable Achievements

On August 8th 2024, we donated $10000 to Children’s Medical Center Plano for cancer and blood disorder patients. What a memorable day! After a year of hard work, we have achieved the first small goal of AYLUS Frisco & Plano through our own efforts: donating $10,000 to the Children’s Medical Center Plano. Every volunteer feels extremely honored! We know that this $10,000 is fundraised by cups of milk tea, egg tarts, bracelets, balloons, cotton candy, face painting, bacon and sausage. Behind every little thing is the support of so many AYLUS members, and the encouragement of neighbors and friends. This is a small goal, but it made us so united that we achieved the goal that seemed impossible to achieve at first. The growth of our volunteers in this process is obvious to all. Thank you to all the participants who have contributed and supported this fundraising archivement. With the community’s continued understanding and support, our AYLUS will definitely get better and better.

 

As of August 2024, we have donated and shipped 6000+ books to supply three K-8 libraries in South Africa, Kenya, Uganda, Ghana, Botswana and Lesotho through the Libraries with Love Project and the African Library Project, which will serve 20000+ students in Africa.

Here it is. These children–and many like them–are why we do this work.

 

Frisco & Plano Branch has collected over 700 All of Us program signups and received a $3000 grant for extraordinary efforts and outcomes from the All of Us Research Program (https://allofus.nih.gov/) sponsor in 2023.

 

Events We do

On May 18, we participated in the East and West festival held by the McKinney Public Library. At the event, we focused mainly on four things: Fundraiser for our 6th African Library by selling jewelry, making balloon animals, face painting, and Chinese calligraphy lessons, all while promoting AYLUS to local communities.

 

On 2024 Spring Festival Gala of the General Association of Henan in Dallas, the organizer invited AYLUS face painting team to paint faces of the 12 Chinese zodiac signs for the performers. It’s our pleasure to serve the community.

PRESIDENT’S VOLUNTEER SERVICE AWARD (PVSA)

Proud to be an official certifying organization for PVSA, we reward all substantial contributions of active members who have accumulated qualified amount of community service hours during our award year (May 1 to next April 30).

 

 

Hours by Award Bronze Silver Gold
Kids (age 5-10) 26–49 50–74 75+
Teens (age 11–15) 50–74 75–99 100+
Young Adults (16-25)* 100–174 175–249 250+

*Age Criteria: For PVSA 2024-2025, you are a Young Adult if your were born on 10/1/2008 or earlier.
